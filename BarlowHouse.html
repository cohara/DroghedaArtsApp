Barlow House


Barlow House on Narrow West Street is a magnificent Georgian building and a prominent land-mark in Drogheda, dating back to 1734. It has a fine pedimented Gibbsian doorcase with the central window above decorated with a segmental pediment. The building has been attributed to both Richard Cassels and Francis Bindon.

In recent times Barlow House was the local garda station but is now one of two centres of the Droichead Arts Centre - the other being on Stockwell Street beside the library where the contemporary gallery, bar/cafe and theatre are located. Today, Barlow House is home to two theatre companies: Upstate Theatre Project &Calipo Theatre and Picture Company and Verba Editing & Development Perspectives NGO. At the back of the premises there are two artist studios, a print studio and a band room. Inside the rooms are bustling daily with children’s music classes, arts classes and drama classes. At night there are many other activities including rehearsals, writers meetings, life drawing sessions, photography classes, music classes and community meetings.
